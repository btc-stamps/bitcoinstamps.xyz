# Where Bitcoin Stamps Live Across Exchanges

Recognition for <EntityMention entity="bitcoin-stamps">Bitcoin Stamps</EntityMention> and the <EntityMention entity="src-20">SRC-20</EntityMention> token standard has grown across the market, but not all of it looks the same. Some venues let you trade the tokens, some let you hold and view them in a wallet, and some just document or track them. Those are genuinely different things, so it's worth laying them out honestly rather than lumping them together.

## Native marketplaces come first

The tokens are traded and indexed natively where the protocol lives. [OpenStamp](https://docs.openstamp.io/) and Stampscan are the SRC-20-native venues: they read the tokens directly from Bitcoin, so what you see there is the chain's own state, not a third party's ledger. If you want the canonical view of an SRC-20 token, that's the layer to trust.

## OKX: wallet indexing (2024)

In February 2024, the OKX Web3 Wallet [added support for the Stamps SRC-20 standard](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2024/02/14/2828748/0/en/Flash-News-OKX-Wallet-Launches-Support-for-Stamps-SRC-20-Bitcoin-Inscriptions-Standard-Enhancing-User-Experience-with-Viewing-and-Transfer-Capabilities.html), letting users hold, view, and transfer SRC-20 tokens. That was the clearest early signal that a major platform took the standard seriously.

One thing to be precise about: this is wallet indexing, not a trading listing. OKX did not open an SRC-20 spot market, and its zero-fee inscriptions marketplace covered other standards, not SRC-20. So "OKX supports SRC-20 in the wallet" is true, and "trade Stamps on OKX" is not.

## Aggregators and education

Price aggregators track the tokens: CoinGecko maintains a [Top SRC-20 Coins category](https://www.coingecko.com/en/categories/src-20), and CoinMarketCap carries price pages for the individual tokens. Binance Academy also documents the ecosystem with explainer articles on [Bitcoin Stamps](https://academy.binance.com/en/articles/what-are-bitcoin-stamps) and [SRC-20 tokens](https://academy.binance.com/en/glossary/src-20-tokens). That's tracking and education, which is useful for discovery, but it isn't a listing or a partnership, and we don't claim it as one.

## BitMart: spot trading for KEVIN and STAMP

The clearest centralized-exchange step came on December 23, 2025, when BitMart opened spot trading for both tokens: [KEVIN/USDT](https://www.bitmart.com/crypto/KEVIN) and [STAMP/USDT](https://www.bitmart.com/crypto/STAMP), in its Meme and Innovation zone (primary-listing announcements for [KEVIN](https://bitmart.zendesk.com/hc/en-us/articles/44674823250971--Primary-Listing-BitMart-Will-List-Bitcoin-Stamps-SRC-20-KEVIN-2025-12-23) and [STAMP](https://bitmart.zendesk.com/hc/en-us/articles/44675420348827--Primary-Listing-BitMart-Will-List-Bitcoin-Stamps-SRC-20-STAMP-2025-12-23)).

Both tokens are listed and tradeable there. We won't dress that up with volume figures, since liquidity on a young pair is thin and comes and goes. The point is simpler: two Bitcoin Stamps SRC-20 tokens, one of them the protocol's founding token, can now be bought and sold on a centralized exchange.

## Why KEVIN carries the story

<EntityMention entity="kevin" variant="cultural">KEVIN</EntityMention> is the first SRC-20 token, and its history is the ecosystem in miniature. It first appeared as art, Stamp #4258 at block 783,718 on April 3, 2023, a homage to Rare Pepe culture that started replicating across the system on its own. <EntityMention entity="arwyn">Arwyn</EntityMention> then gave that energy a permanent form: on May 3, 2023, at block 788,041, <EntityMention entity="kevin" variant="cultural">KEVIN</EntityMention> was deployed as the first SRC-20 token (Stamp #18,516), and it now has more than 2,300 holders.

## An honest read

SRC-20's exchange presence is earlier-stage than some louder inscription standards, and Binance Academy says as much. What's real is a growing, multi-tier recognition: native markets that trade the tokens at the source, wallet indexing at OKX, tracking on the major aggregators, educational coverage, and now spot trading for KEVIN and STAMP on BitMart. Permanent art on Bitcoin's UTXO set is slowly finding its way onto the screens people already use.
